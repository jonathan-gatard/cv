Building a 3D printer

In 2015, I undertook an ambitious project to build my own 3D printer. 3D printer kits were still not widely available at the time, so I had to teach myself and understand everything on my own. My goal was to balance a reasonable cost with satisfactory print quality. That's why I carefully selected each component of the printer, opting for a 3mm steel frame, an Arduino-based controller, threaded rods for the Z-axis, etc.

Improving the 3D printer

Over the years, I have continued to improve my 3D printer.

  • Trapezoidal screws
  • Rigid couplers
  • Bowden extrusion
  • 32-bit SKR board (instead of 8-bit Arduino)
  • TMC2130 motor driver (silent, collision detection, more accurate)
  • High-end hotend
  • Solid state relay for heated bed + 5mm² cables
  • Screen/controller box
  • Cork insulation for heated bed
  • Motor vibration dampers
  • Humidity box for spools

Laser cutter

In 2021, I acquired a blue LED laser at an affordable cost of about 30€ and designed a support to install it on my 3D printer. Since the 3D printer controller is not intended for this purpose, I used the PWM output of the fan in combination with a power circuit based on transistors to dim my laser. It turned out that the laser operation is reversed, requiring a modification of the printer firmware.
