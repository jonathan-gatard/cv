Carnivorous plants terrarium

The terrarium contains: Cephalotus follicularis, Heliamphora ciliata, Heliamphora heterodoxa x minor, Heliamphora nutans, Heliamphora ionasii.
To keep these species healthy, I must:

  • Respect the temperature difference between day and night of 10°C
  • Maintain a humid substrate and air humidity of 80%
  • Properly light for photosynthesis.

The left and right LEDs are controlled via a ZigBee home automation plug
The LED panel is controlled via an ESP32 with infrared LED connected on the GPIO simulating a remote control. I can thus choose the color and intensity.

Carnivorous plants, Terrarium
Bog
The goal is to create an optimal environment for carnivorous plants by reproducing the conditions of their natural habitat:
- acidic pH
- high humidity
- lack of nutrients in the soil
To do this, I used a recycled bathtub, which I buried in my garden. I then added a layer of pumice at the bottom, a geotextile fabric, and filled the bathtub with a volumetric mixture of:
- 60% blonde peat
- 20% perlite
- 20% river sand
Finally, I planted species adapted to these conditions, such as Dionaea, Sarracenia and Drosera.

Snail terrarium

The terrarium contains Archachatina marginata ovum snails which is the largest snail species in the world.

  • Terrarium made : recycled glass, insulating cork, plywood, 3D printed hinges...
  • Regulation : DIY thermostat with heating plates + ventilation system (temperature/humidity management)
  • Light : The snail does not require light. The LEDs are purely aesthetic and are controlled via a ZigBee home automation outlet
